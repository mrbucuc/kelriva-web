# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary:** Heads of Digital Transformation, CTOs, COOs, and C-suite decision-makers at mid-to-large enterprise firms in fintech, ESG, finance, and corporate coaching. They are evaluating AI systems vendors — not buying SaaS, but commissioning bespoke builds. They arrive skeptical, time-poor, and looking for proof before a conversation.

**Secondary:** Operations leads and data managers who influence the buying decision. They need to understand the "how" before recommending Kelriva upward.

**Context of use:** Desktop, working hours. A C-suite buyer who landed from LinkedIn, a referral, or a Google search. They scroll quickly, scan for credibility signals, and decide within 90 seconds whether to read further or bounce.

## Product Purpose

A marketing landing page and lead generation engine for Kelriva AI Limited — a London-based enterprise AI consultancy. The site's one job is to convert a qualified enterprise visitor into a booked discovery call or a direct email inquiry.

Success looks like: visitor lands, sees proof of capability, trusts the firm, and clicks "Book a discovery call" or emails info@kelriva.ai.

## Brand Personality

**Three words:** Precise. Cinematic. Authoritative.

**Voice:** Confident without shouting. Every sentence earns its place. No buzzwords, no hype. The site speaks to peers, not prospects. The tone of a firm that has already delivered — and knows it.

**Emotional goal:** The visitor should feel they are talking to the most capable AI engineering team they have encountered. Not a startup, not a consultant — a builder.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / AI startup templates: hero with gradient text, purple-to-pink gradients, rocket emojis, "10x your workflow" copy, identical feature icon grids.
- Cyberpunk / neon aesthetics: neon-on-black, hacker vibes, crypto-project energy. Wrong trust register for enterprise.
- Corporate-dull defaults: navy + gold, Times New Roman, stock-photo handshakes. Signals 2008, not 2026.
- Overcrowded dashboards masquerading as landing pages: too many numbers, too many sections, no breathing room.

## Design Principles

1. **Proof over promise.** Every claim is backed by a metric, a case study, or a demonstrated outcome. Assertions without evidence are cut.
2. **Warmth in the dark.** The site is dark and cinematic, but not cold or alienating. The palette stays warm (brown-black, not blue-black). Enterprise buyers are humans.
3. **Conviction in every line.** No hedging, no filler. Section headers say something — they are not "What we do" but "Three capabilities. One outcome."
4. **Rhythm through contrast.** Not every section looks the same. Scale, weight, and background micro-variation create a reading experience with pace.
5. **One clear action.** Every section has at most one CTA direction. The site funnels toward one decision: book a call.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. All interactive elements keyboard-reachable. Meaningful alt text on images. Respect prefers-reduced-motion (Framer Motion already used — add variants). Color is not the only carrier of meaning. Minimum 4.5:1 contrast for all body text.
